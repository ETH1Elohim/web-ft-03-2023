Be Your Own Bank

Custodial vs. Non-Custodial Wallets

In traditional finance, we are used to trusting banks. They hold our money and we trust that they will do so.
As time has gone on, specifically since fractional reserve banking was introduced, this method of storing money has become less secure.
Since the creation of bitcoin in 2009, non-custodial wallets have been a thing, allowing individuals to be their own bank.
Non-custodial wallets such as Ledger and MetaMask allow users to store and hold their own digital assets privately because they hold their own private keys.
